Defending

To protect yourself from being destroyed, a tank has armor. The shape, thickness and angle of your armor depends on which tank you are playing. Things like thick armor, sloped turrets, well-angled armor plates and many others can be featured on a tank. However, you can't have a tank that has all these features. The lower glacis plate, rear, side and cupola are easy to penetrate. Thats why you need to know how to protect them. Its called: blocking damage.

How to block damage?
It is based on the rules of penetration mechanics.
You will block a hit if: The armor relative thickness
is higher than the shell's penetration capability.
Or if the shell hit a screen or external module,
lost energy, and failed to penetrate.
Or when a ricochet occurs.
